# TradingView — Common How-To Guide

General rule: most chart appearance settings live in the **Settings** panel — right-click the chart and choose **Settings**, or click the ⚙️ gear icon (top-right of the chart toolbar).

## Change candle / chart colors
1. Right-click anywhere on the chart → **Settings** (or click the ⚙️ gear icon).
2. Go to the **Symbol** tab.
3. You'll see color pickers for **Body**, **Borders**, and **Wick** — with separate colors for **up** (bullish) and **down** (bearish) candles.
4. Click each color swatch, pick your color, then click **OK**.
Tip: toggle the checkboxes next to "Body / Borders / Wick" to turn each element on or off.

## Change the background / grid color
1. ⚙️ Settings → **Canvas** tab.
2. Under **Background**, choose Solid and pick a color (or a gradient).
3. Under **Grid lines**, change or hide the vertical/horizontal grid.

## Change chart type (candles, bars, line, Heikin Ashi, etc.)
- Click the **chart type icon** in the top toolbar (looks like a small candlestick) and pick from the dropdown: Candles, Hollow Candles, Bars, Line, Area, Heikin Ashi, etc.

## Change the timeframe
- Click the timeframe selector at the top-left of the chart (e.g. "15m", "1H", "D") and choose or type a new one (e.g. 1, 5, 15, 60, 240, D, W).

## Add an indicator
1. Click **Indicators** (the "fx" / Indicators button at the top).
2. Search for the indicator name (e.g. "RSI", "Moving Average", "VWAP").
3. Click it to add it to the chart.
4. To edit it, hover the indicator name on the chart → click the ⚙️ gear → adjust inputs/style.

## Save your chart layout
- Click the **cloud / save icon** (top-right) or press **Ctrl/Cmd + S**. Give the layout a name.

## Save a chart template (colors + indicators to reuse)
- Top toolbar → the template/manage icon → **Save Chart Layout as a Template**, then apply it to any chart later.

## Draw a trendline / horizontal line
1. Pick the **line tool** from the left-hand drawing toolbar (trendline, horizontal line, ray, etc.).
2. Click on the chart to place it. Drag the anchor points to adjust.
3. Double-click the line to change its color, thickness, or style.

## Set an alert
1. Right-click on the chart (or on an indicator/price level) → **Add Alert**.
2. Set the condition (crossing, greater than, etc.), the trigger (Once, Every time), and the notification method.
3. Click **Create**.

## Take a screenshot of your chart
- Click the **camera icon** (top-right) → "Copy image" or "Save image", or press **Ctrl/Cmd + Alt + S**.

## Change to dark / light mode
- ⚙️ Settings → **Canvas** and set the background, or use the theme toggle in the main TradingView menu (bottom-left user menu → Theme).

## Reset the chart / remove all drawings
- Right-click the chart → **Remove Drawings** / **Reset Chart**, or use the trash icon on the drawing toolbar.
