# Trading Basics — Reference

This is general educational reference. When the community's own video material (added as other files) covers a topic, prefer that.

## Core terms
- **Candlestick**: shows open, high, low, close for a time period. Green/up = close above open; red/down = close below open. Body = open-to-close; wicks = the highs/lows.
- **Timeframe**: the period each candle represents (1m, 5m, 15m, 1H, 4H, Daily, etc.). Higher timeframes = bigger-picture bias; lower timeframes = entries/timing.
- **Trend**: higher highs + higher lows = uptrend; lower highs + lower lows = downtrend; sideways = range/consolidation.
- **Support**: a price area where buyers tend to step in. **Resistance**: where sellers tend to step in.
- **Bid/Ask & Spread**: bid = highest buyer price, ask = lowest seller price; the gap is the spread.
- **Long / Short**: long = buy expecting price up; short = sell expecting price down.

## Order types
- **Market order**: fills immediately at the current price.
- **Limit order**: fills only at your chosen price or better.
- **Stop order / stop-loss**: triggers when price hits a level — used to cap losses.
- **Take-profit (TP)**: an order to close in profit at a target.

## Risk management (the part that keeps accounts alive)
- **Risk per trade**: many traders risk a small fixed % of the account per trade (commonly ~1%).
- **Stop-loss**: define where you're wrong BEFORE entering, and place a stop there.
- **Risk-to-reward (R:R)**: compare risk to potential reward (e.g. risking 1 to make 2 = 2R). Positive R:R lets you be right less than half the time and still profit.
- **Position sizing**: size the trade so that hitting your stop only loses your intended risk amount.
- **Don't overtrade / revenge trade**: stick to a plan; step away after big wins or losses.

## Common concepts members ask about
- **Liquidity**: areas where lots of orders sit (e.g. above swing highs / below swing lows, round numbers). Price often "seeks" liquidity — moving to areas where stops and pending orders cluster.
- **Order blocks / supply & demand zones**: areas where a strong move originated; price may react there again.
- **Heatmaps**: visualize where resting orders (bids/asks) are concentrated — used to spot liquidity and potential reaction levels.
- **All-time highs (ATH)**: price above all prior history — no overhead resistance from past price; often watched for breakouts and continuation.
- **Market movement / structure**: how price makes and breaks highs and lows; a "break of structure" can signal a trend shift.
- **Session timing**: volatility clusters around market opens (e.g. major stock/futures and FX sessions).

## Chart types worth knowing
- **Candlesticks**: the default; most detail.
- **Heikin Ashi**: smoothed candles that make trends easier to see (but hide exact open/close).
- **Line**: connects closes; good for clean big-picture view.

## Healthy expectations
- No indicator or setup wins every time. Trading is about probabilities and managing risk over many trades.
- Losses are a normal cost of doing business; protecting capital matters more than any single trade.
